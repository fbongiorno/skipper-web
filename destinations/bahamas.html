Cruising and Island Hopping

Comprised of 700 distinct coral islands and thousands of uninhabited cays, the Bahamas are custom-built for island hopping. Unlike deep-water regions, much of the Bahamian archipelago is incredibly shallow, making it the premier global playground for luxury catamarans and shallow-draft motor yachts. Snorkel the vibrant James Bond 'Thunderball Grotto', feed the famous swimming pigs at Big Major Cay, or sail the protected central Sea of Abaco where land is almost always in sight.

Climate and Endless Summers

While the Mediterranean sleeps out the winter, the Bahamas come alive. This is the ultimate winter escape, offering balmy, dry weather from November straight through to late May. The trade winds blow clear and consistently across the Exuma Sound, offering brilliant blue skies and water so stunningly transparent that yachts appear to be suspended in mid-air. Summer months are quieter but significantly hotter, transitioning into the tropical storm season.

A Culture to Remember

Bahamian culture is deeply tied to the rhythm of the sea and the legacy of its people. Nassau provides high-energy Junkanoo festivals, rich colonial history, and sprawling luxury casino resorts like Baha Mar. However, the true soul of the Bahamas is found in the 'Out Islands'—where generations of boat builders, conch divers, and friendly locals operate welcoming beachfront shacks serving up fresh conch salad, cracked lobster, and the beloved Bahama Mama cocktail under the shade of swaying palms.
