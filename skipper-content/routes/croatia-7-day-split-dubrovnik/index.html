7-Day Croatia Sailing Itinerary: Split to Dubrovnik

Sail from Split to Dubrovnik in 7 days. This classic Dalmatian Coast route covers Hvar, Korčula, and Mljet with daily distances and anchorage guides.

Overview

The Split to Dubrovnik route is Croatia's classic sailing itinerary — a week of medieval harbor towns, secluded island bays, and some of the finest seafood in the Adriatic. The route is easily sailed by beginners: passages are short, the maestral afternoon sea breeze is reliable, and the marinas are well-equipped.

Day by day

Day 1 — Split to Hvar (25nm): Leave Split by mid-morning and sail to Hvar Town. If the harbor is full (likely in August), anchor in the bay of Palmižana on Sveti Klement island, just across the channel. Day 2 — Hvar to Korčula (25nm): A leisurely sail along the southern Hvar coast. Korčula Town is a miniature Dubrovnik — a perfectly walled medieval city on a small peninsula. Day 3 — Day in Korčula: Explore the town, rent bicycles, or take a wine tour of the Pelješac Peninsula just across the channel. Day 4 — Korčula to Mljet (30nm): Sail south and enter Mljet National Park through the narrow Soline channel. Anchor in the lake and swim from the boat. Day 5 — Mljet to Šipan (25nm): A quiet island with a wonderful fish restaurant in Suđurađ harbour. Day 6 — Šipan to Dubrovnik (15nm): A short morning sail into Dubrovnik's Gruž harbor. The old city walls are best seen from the water — sail close before mooring. Day 7: Return the boat; depart from Dubrovnik.

Frequently asked questions

How far in advance should I book?
For peak season (July–August in Med, Christmas in Caribbean), book 6+ months ahead. Shoulder season has more flexibility.
Is this route suitable for beginners?
Check the difficulty rating above. Beginner-rated routes have short passages and sheltered conditions. Always have more experience than you think you need.
Can I extend or shorten this itinerary?
Yes — treat this as a framework. Your actual route will depend on weather, pace, and what you discover along the way. Local knowledge from the charter company is invaluable.
