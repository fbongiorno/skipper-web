7-Day Croatia Sailing Itinerary: Split to Dubrovnik

The Split to Dubrovnik route is Croatia's classic sailing itinerary — a week of medieval harbour towns, secluded island bays, and some of the finest seafood in the Adriatic.

Overview

Passages are short, the maestral afternoon sea breeze is reliable, and the marinas are well-equipped. This is Croatia's most popular charter route, and deservedly so.

Day by day

Day 1 — Split to Hvar (25nm). Leave Split by mid-morning. Day 2 — Hvar to Korčula (25nm). A leisurely sail along the southern Hvar coast. Day 3 — Day in Korčula. Wine tour of the Pelješac Peninsula. Day 4 — Korčula to Mljet (30nm). Sail into Mljet National Park. Day 5 — Mljet to Šipan (25nm). Quiet island, excellent fish restaurant. Day 6 — Šipan to Dubrovnik (15nm). Short morning sail. Day 7: Return the boat.

Key anchorages

Palmižana (Hvar), Luka Polače (Mljet), Šipanska Luka (Šipan). Arrive before midday in peak season.

Provisioning & marinas

Provision at ACI Marina Split before departure. Budget $28–$66/night marina fees. The NCI anchorage fee ($17–$28) applies in most national park areas.

Frequently asked questions

How far in advance to book?
For July–August, 6+ months ahead. Shoulder season: 2–3 months.
Is this suitable for beginners?
Yes — the Dalmatian Coast is one of Europe's best beginner sailing routes.
Can I do it in reverse — Dubrovnik to Split?
Yes — most operators allow this. You'll sail more upwind but the wind is generally lighter in September.
