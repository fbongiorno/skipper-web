Whitsundays Sailing Itinerary: 7-Day Route Guide

Seven days in the heart of the Great Barrier Reef — Whitehaven Beach, Hill Inlet's swirling sands, Blue Pearl Bay's coral gardens, and 74 islands of Queensland paradise.

Overview

The Whitsundays sailing circuit covers the highlights of Australia's most spectacular sailing destination in 7 days. The SE trade winds make for comfortable reaching, the anchorages are well-protected, and the sailing distances are manageable. The challenge is choosing what to leave out — there's more to see than a week allows.

Day by day

Day 1 — Airlie Beach to Cid Harbour, Whitsunday Island (25nm): Sail east from the mainland to Whitsunday Island's Cid Harbour — the most protected anchorage in the group, good snorkeling on the fringing reef. Day 2 — Cid Harbour to Whitehaven Beach (10nm): Sail north to the southern end of Whitehaven Beach. Anchor off and dinghy ashore — walk the beach in both directions. Day 3 — Whitehaven to Hill Inlet (5nm): Sail to the northern end of Whitehaven and anchor off. Hike to the Hill Inlet lookout at low tide for the famous swirling sand photograph. Day 4 — Hill Inlet to Blue Pearl Bay, Hayman Island (20nm): One of the Whitsundays' best snorkel sites — coral bommies, reef fish, and excellent visibility. Day 5 — Hayman to Border Island (10nm): The most remote anchorage on the circuit — excellent snorkeling, very few boats even in peak season. Day 6 — Border Island to Hook Island, Butterfly Bay (20nm): Butterfly Bay is a must — anchor in the bay and snorkel the fringing reef, which is among the best coral in the Whitsundays. Day 7 — Hook Island to Airlie Beach (20nm): Return to base via the Whitsunday Passage.

Key anchorages

Cid Harbour — excellent holding, well-protected, 8–12m. Whitehaven Beach — anchor off southern end, 4–6m sand. Blue Pearl Bay — mooring buoys available. Butterfly Bay — anchor 4–6m, fringing reef on north side.

Provisioning & marinas

Provision fully at Airlie Beach (Woolworths 10 minutes from marina) or Hamilton Island (expensive). No provisioning at any anchorage. Fuel at Airlie Beach and Hamilton Island. Budget AUD $35–$65/night.

Frequently asked questions

Do I need a Queensland sailing endorsement?
Most Whitsundays charter companies require an RYA Day Skipper, ASA 104, or equivalent, plus a mandatory skipper briefing. Some operators offer a supervised trial sail as an alternative to formal qualifications.
What is the best month to sail the Whitsundays?
July and August offer the most reliable SE trade winds (15–20 knots) and the clearest visibility. June and September are also excellent with fewer crowds.
Are there dangerous marine animals in the Whitsundays?
Box jellyfish (marine stingers) are present from approximately November through May. Outside this period swimming is completely safe. Reef sharks are common and harmless. Crocodiles are not present in the Whitsunday waters.
