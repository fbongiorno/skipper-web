Sardinia Sailing Circuit: 10-Day Costa Smeralda Route

A 10-day circuit of Sardinia's spectacular northeast coast — the Costa Smeralda, the Maddalena Archipelago, the Strait of Bonifacio, and some of the clearest water in the Mediterranean.

Overview

Sardinia's northeast coast is the Mediterranean at its most spectacular. This 10-day circuit takes in the Costa Smeralda's emerald bays, the Maddalena Archipelago National Park, a day crossing to Bonifacio in Corsica, and a return along Sardinia's dramatic northern coast. It's a route for intermediate sailors who want something beyond the standard Greek island or Croatian circuit.

Day by day

Day 1 — Portisco to Porto Cervo (15nm): Easy first day along the Costa Smeralda. Arrive in Porto Cervo and explore the most glamorous marina in Italy. Day 2 — Porto Cervo to Capriccioli (8nm): A short hop to one of the most beautiful bays on the coast. Anchor and spend the afternoon swimming. Day 3 — Capriccioli to Palau (20nm): Sail north to Palau, the gateway to the Maddalena Archipelago. Day 4 — Palau to Spargi (15nm): Enter the Maddalena Archipelago National Park. Anchor off Spargi island in Cala Corsara — crystalline water over white sand. Day 5 — Spargi to Santa Maria (10nm): Explore the northern islands — Santa Maria, Razzoli. Day 6 — Crossing to Bonifacio (20nm): Cross the Strait of Bonifacio (check wind and current carefully) to Bonifacio in Corsica. Lunch in the cliffside town, return by evening. Day 7 — Maddalena to La Maddalena town (12nm): Visit the main island of La Maddalena — excellent chandlery, provisioning, and a lively town. Day 8 — La Maddalena to Cannigione (25nm): Sail south along the coast to Cannigione bay — excellent anchorage, good local restaurants. Day 9 — Cannigione to Cala di Volpe (20nm): The most famous bay on the Costa Smeralda — anchor off the legendary hotel beach. Day 10 — Cala di Volpe to Portisco (15nm): Return to base via the bay of Porto Rotondo.

Key anchorages

Cala Corsara (Spargi) — crystal-clear, sand bottom, 4–6m, good holding. Cala Santa Maria (Santa Maria island) — beautiful beach, NPS mooring buoys required. Cala di Volpe — glamorous, crowded in August, arrive early. Cannigione Bay — excellent shelter, good holding, town quay available.

Provisioning & marinas

Provision fully at Portisco or Porto Cervo before entering the archipelago — island shopping is limited. Main marinas: Portisco, Porto Cervo (expensive), La Maddalena, Cannigione. Budget $33–$110/night marina fees in peak season.

Frequently asked questions

Do I need a park permit for Maddalena?
Yes — a daily park permit is required for the La Maddalena Archipelago National Park. Available from park wardens or online. Strict anchoring restrictions apply — use the designated mooring buoys.
What are the currents like in the Strait of Bonifacio?
Strong and variable — up to 3 knots. Always check the forecast and cross with a fair current if possible. The mistrale can accelerate dramatically through the strait with little warning.
Is the Sardinia circuit suitable for beginners?
Not recommended for beginners. The Strait of Bonifacio and the summer mistrale require solid intermediate experience. The Costa Smeralda bays are accessible to all levels.
