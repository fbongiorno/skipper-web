Bahamas Sailing Route: 7-Day Exuma Cays Itinerary

From Nassau south through the Exuma Cays — 7 days of the world's most transparent water, the Thunderball Grotto, the swimming pigs of Big Major's Spot, and the extraordinary Exuma Cays Land and Sea Park.

Overview

This southbound itinerary follows the Exuma chain from Nassau to George Town — a one-way charter with a fly-back from Exuma Airport. The Exuma Cays sit on the shallow Bahama Bank; the water is 2–10 feet deep in many places, impossibly clear, and every shade of turquoise imaginable. A shallow-draft vessel (catamaran strongly recommended) is essential.

Day by day

Day 1 — Nassau to Allan's Cay (35nm): Clear customs in Nassau and sail south to Allan's Cay — home of the endangered Bahamian iguana. Beach your dinghy and the iguanas come to you. Day 2 — Allan's Cay to Warderick Wells (20nm): The headquarters of the Exuma Cays Land and Sea Park. Mooring buoys only (no anchoring). Hike to Boo Boo Hill for panoramic views. Day 3 — Warderick Wells to Staniel Cay (15nm): The social hub of the Exumas — Happy People Marina, the Staniel Cay Yacht Club, and the extraordinary Thunderball Grotto (swim-through cave used in two Bond films). Day 4 — Staniel Cay (day trip: Big Major's Spot): Dinghy north 2nm to Big Major's Spot — the famous swimming pigs come out to meet your boat. Back to Staniel Cay for sundowners. Day 5 — Staniel Cay to Black Point (10nm): Traditional Bahamian settlement — excellent local bakery, good snorkeling on the reef. Day 6 — Black Point to Little Farmer's Cay (15nm): The annual Little Farmer's Cay Festival (February) is one of the Bahamas' great events. Ocean Cabin restaurant for lobster. Day 7 — Little Farmer's Cay to George Town (25nm): Arrive in Elizabeth Harbour, George Town — the Exumas' main town. Return boat and fly home from Exuma Airport.

Key anchorages

Allan's Cay — anchor west side, sand bottom, 8–12ft. Warderick Wells — NPS mooring buoys mandatory ($15/night). Staniel Cay — anchor north of Happy People Marina, 6–10ft. Elizabeth Harbour, George Town — huge anchorage, excellent holding.

Provisioning & marinas

Provision fully in Nassau before departure — Bahamas Trading is well-stocked. Limited provisioning at Staniel Cay (expensive) and George Town (good). Fuel available at Staniel Cay and George Town. Budget $28–$66/night.

Frequently asked questions

Is the Exumas suitable for beginner sailors?
The Exuma Bank passages are relatively short, but tidal cuts require careful navigation and the shallow water requires vigilance for coral heads. Intermediate experience recommended; a catamaran is strongly preferred.
What is the Bahamas Cruising Permit?
All vessels must obtain a Bahamas Cruising Permit on entry ($300 for vessels under 35ft). This covers the entire stay. Clear customs at Nassau, Marsh Harbour, or George Town on arrival.
Can I anchor in the Exuma Cays Land and Sea Park?
No anchoring is permitted within the park boundaries — mooring buoys only. Book via the Bahamas National Trust app. No fishing or collecting of any kind is permitted within park boundaries.
