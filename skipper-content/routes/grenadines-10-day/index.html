Grenadines Sailing Route: 10-Day St Lucia to Grenada Itinerary

The Grenadines from north to south — 10 days of steady trade winds, Tobago Cays snorkeling, Bequia's rum punches, and Grenada's spice markets at the turnaround.

Overview

This itinerary runs south — downwind with the trade winds, the classic direction for Grenadines sailing. Fly into St Lucia, charter from Rodney Bay Marina, and spend 10 days working south through the most spectacular island chain in the Caribbean. Fly home from Grenada.

Day by day

Day 1 — Rodney Bay, St Lucia (base): Provision, brief, and spend the first night at the marina. Day 2 — Rodney Bay to Bequia (45nm): The longest passage of the trip — an exhilarating trade-wind sail. Anchor in Admiralty Bay. Day 3 — Bequia: Rest day. Explore Port Elizabeth, snorkel the reefs, visit the Frangipani bar. Day 4 — Bequia to Mustique (8nm): Short sail to Mustique. Anchor in Britannia Bay, visit Basil's Bar. Day 5 — Mustique to Canouan (18nm): Sail south to Canouan — quieter and less visited, excellent snorkeling on the northern reef. Day 6 — Canouan to Tobago Cays (15nm): Arrive at the crown jewel. Anchor inside the horseshoe reef and snorkel all afternoon. Day 7 — Tobago Cays: Full day in the marine park — turtles, coral gardens, the uninhabited beaches. Day 8 — Tobago Cays to Union Island (8nm): Clifton Harbour, Union Island — good chandlery and a lively rum bar scene. Day 9 — Union Island to Carriacou (15nm): Cross into Grenadian waters. Hillsborough Bay, Carriacou — traditional boat-building and excellent lobster. Day 10 — Carriacou to St George's, Grenada (35nm): Final day's sail to the Spice Isle. St George's harbour is spectacular.

Key anchorages

Admiralty Bay, Bequia — excellent holding, 5–8m, crowded but manageable. Britannia Bay, Mustique — mooring buoys only (mandatory). Tobago Cays — NPS mooring buoys inside the reef ($10/night). Hillsborough Bay, Carriacou — good holding, 4–6m, clear of ferry traffic.

Provisioning & marinas

Provision fully at Rodney Bay (Massy Superstore — excellent) before departure. Restock at Bequia (good) and Union Island (basic). Grenada has excellent provisioning on return. Budget $44–$88/night mooring/marina fees.

Frequently asked questions

How difficult is the St Lucia to Bequia passage?
It's the longest passage of the itinerary at 45nm and involves crossing the St Vincent Channel — one of the windier passages in the Eastern Caribbean (typically 20–28 knots SE). It's a bracing but manageable day sail for experienced crews. Leave by 0700.
Do I need to clear customs between islands?
Yes — customs are required at each island nation. St Lucia, St Vincent & the Grenadines, and Grenada are three separate countries. The process is straightforward at each; carry multiple copies of crew list and ship's papers.
Is a catamaran recommended for the Grenadines?
Yes — for groups of 6+ and for sailing with children, a catamaran is strongly recommended. The trade wind passages are more comfortable, the shallow draft accesses more anchorages, and the platform stability is appreciated in the rolling swells.
