Overview

The Cyclades circuit is one of sailing's great itineraries — a week of island-hopping through some of the most iconic scenery in the Mediterranean. Starting from Lavrion (Athens), this route visits Kea, Syros, Mykonos, Naxos, Paros, and Santorini before returning. Expect strong meltemi winds in July and August; consider doing this route in May, June, or September for more relaxed conditions.

Day by day

Day 1 — Athens (Lavrion) to Kea (25nm): An easy first day's sail across the Saronic Gulf. Arrive in Kea's main port, Korissia, and explore the island's distinctive ancient remains. Day 2 — Kea to Syros (45nm): The longest passage of the route; leave early to make Ermoupolis (Syros) by afternoon. Syros is the Cyclades capital — a magnificent neoclassical town that most island-hoppers miss entirely. Day 3 — Syros to Mykonos (25nm): An easy morning sail to Mykonos. Arrive early to anchor in Ornos bay; move to the marina if you want the full Mykonos nightlife experience. Day 4 — Mykonos to Naxos (35nm): A classic day sail across the central Cyclades. Naxos is the largest island in the group with the most authentic local life. Day 5 — Naxos to Paros (10nm): A short hop to Paros — enjoy the extra time exploring Parikia's old town or taking the bus across to the beautiful beach village of Naoussa. Day 6 — Paros to Santorini (50nm): The longest day's sail, with the reward of arriving at the caldera anchorage as the sun sets. Anchor in Ormos Athinios or Fira. Day 7 — Santorini to Athens (130nm): Too far to sail in a day; either overnight passage or arrange a one-way charter to leave the boat in Santorini.

Frequently asked questions

How far in advance should I book?
For peak season (July–August in Med, Christmas in Caribbean), book 6+ months ahead. Shoulder season has more flexibility.
Is this route suitable for beginners?
Check the difficulty rating above. Beginner-rated routes have short passages and sheltered conditions. Always have more experience than you think you need.
Can I extend or shorten this itinerary?
Yes — treat this as a framework. Your actual route will depend on weather, pace, and what you discover along the way. Local knowledge from the charter company is invaluable.
