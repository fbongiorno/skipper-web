7-Day Greek Islands Sailing Itinerary (Cyclades Circuit)

The Cyclades circuit is one of sailing's great itineraries — a week of island-hopping through some of the most iconic scenery in the Mediterranean.

Overview

Starting from Lavrion (Athens), this route visits Kea, Syros, Mykonos, Naxos, Paros, and Santorini. Expect strong meltemi winds in July and August; consider May, June, or September for more relaxed conditions.

Day by day

Day 1 — Athens to Kea (25nm): An easy first day across the Saronic Gulf. Day 2 — Kea to Syros (45nm): Leave early — the longest passage of the route. Syros is the Cyclades capital, a magnificent neoclassical town. Day 3 — Syros to Mykonos (25nm): Easy morning sail. Anchor in Ornos bay. Day 4 — Mykonos to Naxos (35nm): Classic Cyclades day sail. Day 5 — Naxos to Paros (10nm): Short hop — use the extra time exploring. Day 6 — Paros to Santorini (50nm): Arrive at the caldera as the sun sets. Day 7: Either overnight passage back to Athens or arrange a one-way charter.

Key anchorages

Ornos bay (Mykonos), Ormos Pisses (Kea), Livadi (Serifos), Ormos Athinios (Santorini). Arrive before noon in July and August to secure an anchorage.

Provisioning & marinas

Provision fully at Lavrion before departure — island supermarkets are expensive. Main marinas: Lavrion, Mykonos, Naxos. Budget $33–$88/night marina fees.

Frequently asked questions

How far in advance should I book?
For July–August, book 6+ months ahead. Shoulder season has more availability.
Is this suitable for beginners?
The Cyclades is rated intermediate due to the meltemi. Beginners should consider the Ionian Islands instead.
Can I extend the itinerary?
Yes — add Ios or Folegandros between Naxos and Santorini for a more leisurely pace.
