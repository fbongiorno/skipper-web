Overview

A two-week passage from southern Italy to the Greek Ionian islands is one of the Mediterranean's great sailing adventures. This itinerary takes you from the baroque splendor of Naples via the Amalfi Coast, across the heel of Italy, through the Ionian to Corfu. It works as a one-way charter; arrange to leave the boat in Corfu and fly home.

Day by day (summary)

Days 1–3 — Amalfi Coast: Depart Salerno, sail to Positano, Amalfi, and anchor off Capri before heading south. Days 4–5 — Calabria: Down the Italian toe — stop at Tropea, one of Italy's most beautiful cliff-top towns. Day 6 — Strait of Messina: The famous strait between Sicily and mainland Italy. Time the tidal stream carefully and go through at slack water. Days 7–8 — Crossing to Greece: Overnight or two-day passage across the Ionian Sea to Corfu or the Ionian islands. Days 9–14 — Greek Ionian: Explore Corfu, Paxos, Lefkada, and the southern Ionian islands at leisure.

Frequently asked questions

How far in advance should I book?
For peak season (July–August in Med, Christmas in Caribbean), book 6+ months ahead. Shoulder season has more flexibility.
Is this route suitable for beginners?
Check the difficulty rating above. Beginner-rated routes have short passages and sheltered conditions. Always have more experience than you think you need.
Can I extend or shorten this itinerary?
Yes — treat this as a framework. Your actual route will depend on weather, pace, and what you discover along the way. Local knowledge from the charter company is invaluable.
