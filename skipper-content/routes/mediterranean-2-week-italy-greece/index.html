2-Week Mediterranean Sailing Itinerary: Italy to Greece

A two-week passage from southern Italy to the Greek Ionian islands is one of the Mediterranean's great sailing adventures — from the Amalfi Coast to Corfu.

Overview

This itinerary takes you from the baroque splendor of Naples via the Amalfi Coast, across the heel of Italy, through the Strait of Messina, and across the Ionian Sea to Corfu.

Day by day (summary)

Days 1–3 — Amalfi Coast: Depart Salerno, sail to Positano, Amalfi, anchor off Capri. Days 4–5 — Calabria: Down the Italian toe — stop at Tropea, one of Italy's most beautiful cliff-top towns. Day 6 — Strait of Messina: Time the tidal stream carefully, go through at slack water. Days 7–8 — Crossing to Greece: Overnight or two-day passage across the Ionian Sea. Days 9–14 — Greek Ionian: Explore Corfu, Paxos, Lefkada, and the southern Ionian at leisure.

Key passages

The Strait of Messina requires careful timing — tidal streams run at up to 3 knots. The Ionian crossing (60–80nm) is best made with a settled weather forecast; the Ionian Sea can produce short, steep seas in strong winds.

Frequently asked questions

Is this suitable for beginners?
This is an intermediate route due to the offshore crossing. Beginners should have 500+ miles of experience.
Can I do this as a one-way charter?
Yes — arrange to leave the boat in Corfu and fly home. Most operators allow one-way for a repositioning fee.
What is the best time of year?
May and June are ideal — the crossing is calmer before summer heat builds the Ionian's afternoon thermals.
