Sailing in Spain: Balearic Islands & Costa del Sol Guide

From the party islands of Ibiza to the sheltered bays of Mallorca's northern coast, Spain offers some of the most varied sailing in the Mediterranean — with world-class marinas and a nightlife scene that extends well past sundown.

Overview

Spain's sailing is defined by two very different worlds: the Balearic Islands — Mallorca, Ibiza, Menorca, and Formentera — floating in the western Mediterranean, and the Costa del Sol stretching along the Andalusian coast toward Gibraltar. Both offer excellent sailing, but the Balearics are the clear headline act.

Balearic Islands

The Balearics are the Mediterranean's premier sailing archipelago outside of Greece. Four main islands, each with a distinct character, within easy day-sailing distance of each other. Mallorca anchors the fleet with excellent marinas, stunning northern coast scenery, and easy access to the others. Menorca — the quietest and least developed — offers the finest anchorages of the group and a UNESCO Biosphere Reserve designation. Ibiza blends legendary nightlife with surprisingly beautiful sailing in its southern coves. Formentera, Ibiza's tiny southern neighbor, has arguably the finest beaches in Spain.

Mallorca sailing

Palma de Mallorca is one of the Mediterranean's great sailing cities — a stunning bay, excellent marinas (including the famous Club de Mar), and a superyacht scene that rivals Monaco in July. The northern coast between Port de Sóller and Cap Formentor is among the most dramatic sailing in Spain: sheer limestone cliffs plunging to turquoise water, with a handful of small anchorages accessible only by boat.

Ibiza & Formentera

Ibiza's southern coast — Es Canar, Cala Bassa, Cala Compte — offers beautiful anchorages with a completely different vibe from the northern clubs. Arrive early and anchor in the coves; by midday the water taxis and day charter boats arrive. A downwind run to Formentera's Illetes beach — barely a sandbar with hypnotic aquamarine water — is one of the Mediterranean's finest day sails.

Costa del Sol

The Costa del Sol — Marbella, Estepona, Puerto Banús — is superyacht and motor yacht territory, but well-equipped marinas make it viable for sailing charters too. The proximity to Gibraltar and the Atlantic adds an interesting dimension; many blue-water passages begin here with a transit of the Strait.

Frequently asked questions

Which is better for sailing — Mallorca or Ibiza?
Mallorca offers more varied sailing, better infrastructure, and more anchorages. Ibiza is better if you want to combine sailing with the island's nightlife scene.
Is Menorca good for sailing?
Menorca is excellent — the quietest and most natural of the Balearics, with the best anchorages and least crowded waters. Highly recommended for sailors who want to escape the crowds.
Can I sail from Spain to Morocco?
Yes, the crossing from Tarifa or Algeciras to Ceuta or Tangier is around 15–20 miles. It requires advance planning and is subject to strong tidal streams in the Strait of Gibraltar.
