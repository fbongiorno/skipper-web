When to Choose the Mediterranean

The Mediterranean is for sailors who want civilisation alongside their sailing. No other sailing region puts you within reach of Santorini's caldera sunsets, Dubrovnik's medieval walls, Amalfi Coast villages, and Istanbul's skyline - often on the same trip. The density of historical sites, excellent restaurants, and diverse cultures makes every port a destination in itself.

When to Choose the Caribbean

The Caribbean is for sailors who want picture-perfect conditions and stunning natural beauty with minimal stress. The northeast trades blow reliably all winter at 15-20 knots, creating ideal beam reaching conditions. The water colour is extraordinary, the beaches are white sand perfection, and the beach bar culture is like nowhere else on Earth.

The Verdict

Choose the Mediterranean if: You want to combine world-class sailing with history, culture, and cuisine. Best for European sailors and anyone who values variety.

Choose the Caribbean if: You want reliable trade winds, white sand beaches, crystal clear water, and a relaxed tropical atmosphere. Best for North American sailors and families.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I do both the Mediterranean and Caribbean in one year?

Absolutely. Many sailors keep their boat in the Mediterranean May-October, then cross the Atlantic for the Caribbean winter season November-April. The Atlantic crossing itself takes 2-3 weeks and is a highlight.

Which is better for beginner sailors?

The Caribbean, specifically the BVI or Grenadines. Consistent trade winds, short passages, and flat water make it significantly more forgiving than the Mediterranean.
