Yachting the Bahamas: Complete Island-Hopping Guide (2026)

Seven hundred islands of flat, sun-bleached paradise with the most transparent water on earth — the Bahamas is a cruising ground unlike anywhere else, combining easy sailing with extraordinary natural beauty.

Overview

The Bahamas archipelago stretches 760 miles from Grand Bahama (100 miles east of Florida) to the Turks and Caicos. It's one of the world's most varied cruising grounds — from the shallow turquoise flats of the Exumas (where you can see the sandy bottom in 20 feet of water) to the bluewater ocean passages of the outer islands. The defining characteristic is the water: nowhere else on earth is the ocean this clear. The Atlantic side is deep blue; the Bahama Bank is every shade of turquoise imaginable.

The Exumas

The Exuma Cays are the highlight of Bahamas sailing — a 120-mile chain of small cays and islands with the most extraordinary water color in the Caribbean. The Exuma Cays Land and Sea Park (anchoring only, no fishing) has the best snorkeling in the Bahamas. Staniel Cay is the social hub with the famous Thunderball Grotto (where James Bond films were shot). Compass Cay has friendly nurse sharks you can swim with. Big Major's Spot is home to the famous swimming pigs. Charter from Nassau (2–3 hour sail south) or fly into George Town (Exuma) for a one-way charter north.

The Abacos

The Abacos in the north are the traditional Bahamas sailing destination — the Sea of Abaco is a sheltered inland sea providing excellent protected sailing between the outer barrier cays. Marsh Harbour is the main charter base; Hope Town on Elbow Cay (with its famous candy-striped lighthouse) is the most photographed harbour in the Bahamas. The Abacos are particularly popular with Florida sailors making their first offshore passage.

Nassau & New Providence

Nassau is the arrival point for most Bahamas charters — international flights, large marina facilities (Nassau Harbour Club, Atlantis marina), and immediate access south to the Exumas or north to the Abacos. Nassau itself rewards a day's exploration — the colonial architecture, the straw market, and the spectacular Atlantis resort on Paradise Island are all within walking distance of the marina.

Best time to go

November through May is the sailing season. December through March is peak — northeast trade winds of 15–20 knots, clear skies, and temperatures of 24–27°C. April and May are slightly lighter wind but warm and uncrowded. The Bahamas sit north of the main hurricane track but are not immune — avoid June through October.

Charter information

Main charter bases: Nassau and Marsh Harbour (Abacos). Major operators include Sunsail, The Moorings, and Florida-based charter companies running day-hop itineraries. A 40ft bareboat runs $3,900–$6,600/week; catamarans from $6,600. Note: Bahamas waters are very shallow in places (2–6 feet on the Banks) — a shallow-draft vessel or catamaran is strongly recommended over a deep-keel monohull for the Exumas.

Frequently asked questions

Do I need to clear customs in the Bahamas?
Yes — all vessels entering the Bahamas must clear customs, either at a Port of Entry (Nassau, Marsh Harbour, George Town) or using the Bahamas online customs system (BEPAS). The Bahamas Cruising Permit costs $300 for vessels under 35ft, $600 for larger vessels.
Is the Bahamas good for beginner sailors?
The Abacos (sheltered Sea of Abaco) are excellent for beginners. The Exumas require more care due to shallow tidal cuts and coral heads — intermediate experience recommended.
Can I sail to the Bahamas from Florida?
Yes — the Florida-to-Bahamas crossing (Fort Lauderdale to Nassau or Bimini) is about 55nm and one of the most popular offshore passages in the world. Many sailors make the crossing in a day. A well-equipped boat and a good weather window are essential.
What are the swimming pigs of Big Major's Spot?
A famous colony of feral pigs that swim out to greet visiting boats in a cove on Big Major's Spot island in the Exumas. They've become the most photographed attraction in the Bahamas. Anchor off and dinghy in — the pigs come to you.
