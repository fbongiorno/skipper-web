Overview

The Caribbean is defined by its northeast trade winds — steady, reliable, warm — blowing 15–25 knots across the Atlantic. The Caribbean arc stretches over 4,000km from the Bahamas to Trinidad, encompassing hundreds of islands with radically different characters.

British Virgin Islands

The BVI are the world's most popular bareboat charter destination. Short passages, excellent infrastructure, and the sheltered Sir Francis Drake Channel make this ideal for a first charter.

The Grenadines

For many experienced sailors, the Grenadines represent the Caribbean at its finest — spectacular scenery from the volcanic peaks of the Windwards to the palm-fringed Tobago Cays.

Windward Islands

Martinique, St. Lucia, St. Vincent, and Grenada — French-influenced, lushly volcanic, and far less charter-saturated than the BVI. St. Lucia's Rodney Bay is a major charter base.

Best time to go

The Caribbean sailing season runs mid-November through late April. December through February offers the most reliable trade winds.

Frequently asked questions

Which Caribbean island is best for beginners?
The BVI is widely recommended for first-time charter sailors — short passages, steady winds, excellent infrastructure.
Do I need to clear customs between islands?
Most islands require clearing customs on arrival and departure. The BVI is an exception between its own islands.
Is the Caribbean expensive?
Charter rates are higher than the Mediterranean, but provisioning and marina fees are comparable.
