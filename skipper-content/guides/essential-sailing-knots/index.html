The bowline

The bowline (boh-lin) is the most important knot in sailing — a fixed loop that won't slip or jam. Remember: "the rabbit comes up through the hole, runs around the tree, and goes back down the hole."

Cleat hitch

Used to secure a line to a cleat. Take a full turn around the base, make figure-of-eight turns over the horns, finish with a locking hitch.

Round turn & two half hitches

Used to secure a line to a ring, rail, or post — especially useful for attaching fenders.

Reef knot

Used for tying reef points in a sail. Right over left, left over right. Note: not a general-purpose joining knot.

Figure-of-eight stopper knot

Tied at the end of a sheet or halyard to prevent it running through a block. Essential — without it, a line can disappear aloft at the worst moment.

Frequently asked questions

What is the most important knot first?
The bowline. It creates a secure, fixed loop and has dozens of applications aboard.
How do I remember the bowline?
The rabbit comes up through the hole, runs around the tree, and goes back down the hole.
Is the reef knot the same as a square knot?
Yes — also called a square knot in North America.
