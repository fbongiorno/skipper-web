Bareboat Charter License Requirements: What You Need to Know

Booking your first bareboat charter? One of the most common questions is what qualifications you actually need. The answer varies by country, charter company, and boat size — here's a clear breakdown.

Do you need a license to charter a bareboat?

The short answer is: it depends. There is no universal international sailing licence, and requirements vary significantly between countries and charter companies. Some destinations (the BVI, most Caribbean islands) have no formal government requirement at all — charter companies set their own standards. Others (Croatia, Greece, most of Europe) require a recognised national or international qualification.

In practice, most charter companies worldwide require either a recognised qualification like the RYA Day Skipper or ASA 104, or a detailed sailing resume demonstrating equivalent experience — typically 500–1,000 offshore miles as skipper. Some also ask for a VHF radio operator's certificate (Short Range Certificate or SRC).

RYA qualifications

The RYA (Royal Yachting Association) issues the most widely recognised sailing qualifications worldwide. For bareboat chartering, the key certificates are:

  • RYA Day Skipper (Practical) — the standard bareboat entry requirement, accepted worldwide. Covers coastal passage planning, pilotage, and boat handling.
  • RYA Coastal Skipper (Practical) — required for overnight passages and offshore sailing by some operators.
  • RYA Offshore/Ocean Skipper — for blue-water passage making.

RYA courses are run by accredited training centres worldwide. The Day Skipper practical course typically takes 5 days aboard, with an optional shorebased theory component.

ASA qualifications

The ASA (American Sailing Association) system is the primary qualification framework in the United States and is widely accepted in the Caribbean. The equivalent bareboat qualification is:

  • ASA 101 — Basic Keelboat Sailing (prerequisite)
  • ASA 103 — Basic Coastal Cruising
  • ASA 104 — Bareboat Cruising (the standard charter requirement)

ICC (International Certificate of Competence)

The ICC is a UN-backed certificate recognised across Europe, including Croatia, Greece, Italy, France, and Spain. It's not a training certificate — it's issued on the basis of an existing national qualification or practical test. UK sailors with an RYA Helmsman's Certificate or Day Skipper can obtain an ICC directly from the RYA.

Country-specific rules

  • Greece: ICC or national equivalent required. VHF SRC also required.
  • Croatia: ICC or national equivalent + VHF SRC required by law.
  • Turkey: ICC or Turkish national certificate. Gulet charters handled by licensed operators.
  • BVI / Caribbean: No government requirement — charter company standard applies.
  • France: ICC required for inland and coastal waters.

If you don't have a qualification

Several options exist for sailors who want to charter without a formal certificate:

  • Flotilla sailing — join a guided group of yachts with a lead boat and professional skipper. Excellent for learning while chartering.
  • Hire a skipper — most charter companies can arrange a qualified skipper for an additional €150–€250/day. You sail, they take legal responsibility.
  • Take a course first — an RYA Day Skipper practical course takes 5 days and opens up charter opportunities worldwide. Easily the best long-term investment.

Frequently asked questions

Is the RYA Day Skipper accepted in Croatia?
Yes. Croatia requires an ICC, which UK sailors with an RYA Day Skipper (practical) can obtain directly from the RYA. Non-UK sailors should check if their national qualification qualifies for ICC endorsement.
Do I need a VHF radio licence to charter?
Most European countries require a Short Range Certificate (SRC) VHF radio operator's licence to operate a boat's radio legally. It's a short online or in-person course. Strongly recommended even where not compulsory.
Can I charter in the BVI without any certificate?
Yes — the BVI has no government requirement. Charter companies set their own standards: most require either a certificate or a resume of experience (typically 500+ miles as skipper), plus references.
How long does an RYA Day Skipper course take?
The practical component is 5 days aboard. The shorebased theory course (recommended but not mandatory) is around 40 hours of study, usually done online or over winter evenings.
