Bareboat Charter License Requirements: What You Need to Know

Booking your first bareboat charter? One of the most common questions is what qualifications you actually need. The answer varies by country, charter company, and boat size.

Do you need a license?

There is no universal international sailing licence. Requirements vary significantly between countries and charter companies. In practice, most charter companies worldwide require either a recognised qualification like the RYA Day Skipper or ASA 104, or a detailed sailing resume demonstrating equivalent experience.

RYA qualifications

The RYA Day Skipper (Practical) is the standard bareboat entry requirement, accepted worldwide. It covers coastal passage planning, pilotage, and boat handling. The practical course typically takes 5 days aboard.

ASA qualifications

The ASA 104 (Bareboat Cruising) is the standard charter requirement in North America and widely accepted in the Caribbean.

ICC (International Certificate of Competence)

The ICC is a UN-backed certificate recognised across Europe — Greece, Croatia, Turkey, France, and Spain. UK sailors with an RYA Day Skipper can obtain an ICC directly from the RYA.

Country-specific rules

  • Greece: ICC or national equivalent + VHF SRC
  • Croatia: ICC or national equivalent + VHF SRC required by law
  • Turkey: ICC or Turkish national certificate
  • BVI/Caribbean: No government requirement — charter company standard applies

If you don't have a qualification

Flotilla sailing — join a guided group with a lead boat. Hire a skipper — $165–$275/day. Take a course first — an RYA Day Skipper takes 5 days and opens charter opportunities worldwide.

Frequently asked questions

Is RYA Day Skipper accepted in Croatia?
Yes. Croatia requires an ICC, which UK sailors with an RYA Day Skipper can obtain directly from the RYA.
Do I need a VHF licence?
Most European countries require a Short Range Certificate (SRC). Strongly recommended even where not compulsory.
Can I charter in the BVI without any cert?
Yes — the BVI has no government requirement.
