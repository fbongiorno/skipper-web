Overview

Unlike the Mediterranean, the Maldives has very few traditional marinas. Charters are based around Malé, the capital, with most boats — typically crewed catamarans — anchoring at sandbars, reefs, and resort islands rather than using marina berths.

Malé & Hulhumalé

Malé and the reclaimed island of Hulhumalé, beside the international airport, form the main hub. Charters provision and depart here. The transfer from the airport to your boat is quick, and most crews handle all logistics.

How charter works here

Because there are few marinas, the model is different: you board a fully provisioned, crewed catamaran and live aboard, moving between atolls and anchoring each night. Resort marinas exist but are private; most cruising is anchorage-to-anchorage.

Practicalities

Fuel and water are managed by the crew, often topped up at resort islands. Independent provisioning is limited, which is another reason crewed charter — with an onboard cook — dominates the Maldives.

Frequently asked questions

Are there marinas in the Maldives?
Very few. Most charters anchor at sandbars and reefs rather than berthing in marinas.
Where do charters depart from?
Malé, beside the international airport. Crews handle provisioning and logistics.
Can I bareboat in the Maldives?
Rarely — the remoteness and reef navigation make crewed charter the standard.
