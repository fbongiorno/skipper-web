Overview

The Mediterranean sailing season runs from April through October, but the experience varies dramatically month to month. Understanding the seasonal patterns — wind, weather, crowds, and prices — lets you choose the window that suits you best.

May & June

The Mediterranean's best-kept secret. Water temperatures reach 20–22°C by June — warm enough to swim comfortably. Winds are moderate and variable; in the Aegean, the meltemi hasn't built to its summer intensity. Anchorages are quiet, prices are at their lowest, and the hillsides are still green. Highly recommended for both beginners and experienced sailors.

July & August

Peak season. Hot, busy, and in the Aegean, windy. The meltemi blows force 4–6 most afternoons in the Cyclades — excellent for confident sailors, challenging for beginners. Popular anchorages like Mykonos and Santorini are crowded; prices are 30–50% higher than shoulder season. The Ionian remains calmer; Croatia's sheltered coast sees less meltemi effect.

September & October

September is arguably the best month in the Mediterranean. The meltemi eases, water temperature is at its warmest (24–26°C), crowds thin out, and prices drop. October brings occasional depressions from the Atlantic; weather is less reliable but still largely good. The season officially ends in late October for most charter fleets.

Frequently asked questions

Is it safe to sail the Mediterranean in winter?
Most charter fleets are laid up November through March; the few boats available require significantly more passage-planning experience. Winter can bring strong mistral or tramontana winds with little warning.
What wind should I expect in the Cyclades in summer?
The meltemi — a dry northerly thermal wind — blows force 3–6 most afternoons in July and August. It typically builds after midday and eases at night. Morning departures are recommended.
Is October a good time to sail in Greece?
Yes — generally warm, quiet, and excellent value. Weather is less predictable than summer but still largely settled. Water temperature is at its annual peak (24–26°C). Insurance and fleet availability may be limited after mid-October.
