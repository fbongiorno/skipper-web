Overview

The Mediterranean sailing season runs from April through October, but the experience varies dramatically month to month. Understanding the seasonal patterns — wind, weather, crowds, and prices — lets you choose the best window.

May & June

The Mediterranean's best-kept secret. Water temperatures reach 20–22°C by June. Winds are moderate; in the Aegean, the meltemi hasn't built to its summer intensity. Anchorages are quiet, prices are at their lowest. Highly recommended for all levels.

July & August

Peak season. Hot, busy, and in the Aegean, windy. The meltemi blows force 4–6 most afternoons in the Cyclades. Popular anchorages are crowded; prices are 30–50% higher than shoulder season.

September & October

September is arguably the best month in the Mediterranean. The meltemi eases, water temperature is at its warmest (24–26°C), crowds thin, and prices drop. October brings occasional Atlantic depressions; weather is less reliable but still largely good.

Frequently asked questions

Is it safe to sail the Med in winter?
Most charter fleets are laid up November–March. Winter can bring strong mistral or tramontana winds with little warning.
What wind should I expect in the Cyclades in summer?
The meltemi blows force 3–6 most afternoons in July and August. Morning departures are recommended.
Is October a good time to sail in Greece?
Generally warm, quiet, and excellent value. Water temperature is at its annual peak (24–26°C).
