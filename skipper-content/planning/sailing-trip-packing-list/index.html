Overview

Packing for a sailing trip is an exercise in ruthless editing. Space is limited aboard a charter yacht — each cabin typically has one large locker and minimal shelf space. The golden rule: bring a soft holdall, not a rigid suitcase (it stores flat in a locker), and pack for the conditions you'll actually encounter, not the hotel holiday you're used to.

Clothing

Deck shoes (non-slip, white or non-marking sole) are essential — and your yacht-shoes only, not your shore shoes. Bring a waterproof jacket even in summer: Mediterranean thunderstorms arrive fast and a night watch in the Aegean is colder than you expect. A fleece or mid-layer for evenings. Light, quick-dry shorts and shirts for days. Smart-casual clothes for harbour restaurants — in Greece and Croatia, you can go from swimwear to a waterside dinner in 20 minutes.

Safety & sun

High-SPF sunscreen in quantity — you'll use far more than on a beach holiday; the combination of reflection off the water and sea breeze making you feel cool means you'll burn faster. Polarised sunglasses for watch-keeping. A hat with a brim. Lip balm with SPF. Seasickness tablets — take them before you feel ill; they're largely ineffective once nausea sets in. The best prophylactic is a skin patch (Scopolamine/Hyoscine) applied 4+ hours before departure.

Electronics

A waterproof phone case is essential — salt spray damages phones. A portable power bank keeps devices charged between marina stops. If you're navigating, a tablet with Navionics installed is a valuable backup to the chart plotter. Waterproof action camera for underwater shots.

What not to bring

Hard suitcases — they don't fit in lockers. High heels — catastrophically dangerous on a moving deck. Excessive jewellery. Aerosol cans — they roll and are a nuisance. Heavy books — download them instead.

Frequently asked questions

Can I hire sailing gear at the charter base?
Some charter companies offer foul-weather gear hire. It's inconsistent quality — if you plan to sail regularly, buying your own is worthwhile.
How much luggage can I bring on a charter yacht?
Most charter companies recommend a maximum of one 60-litre soft bag per person. In practice, you'll use less space than you think — life aboard is liberating in its simplicity.
Do I need my own lifejacket?
Charter yachts supply lifejackets; you don't need to bring your own. A personal EPIRB or PLB is worth considering for offshore passages.
