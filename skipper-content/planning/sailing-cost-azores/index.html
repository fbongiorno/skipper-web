Overview

As part of Portugal, the Azores are more affordable than Norway or the Maldives. Charter rates are moderate and Portuguese provisioning costs are reasonable, making it good value for such a distinctive destination.

Charter rates

A bareboat monohull runs $3,000–$6,000 per week; catamarans more. Given the open-ocean passages, many charterers add a skipper (~$1,300/week). The small fleet means early booking is important.

Running costs

Portuguese provisioning is reasonable — budget around $70 per person per day. Marina fees average $45 per night, and fuel for a week of inter-island sailing runs around $300–$400. Whale-watching excursions are an easy add-on.

Sample weekly budget

For 6 people on a bareboat monohull: charter $4,500, food $2,940, marina $315, fuel $350 — roughly $8,105 total, about $1,350 per person. Add a skipper for ~$215 per person.

Value notes

The Azores are already one of Europe's better-value cruising grounds — mainland-Portuguese supermarket prices, honest marina fees, and no Mediterranean-style super-tourism premium. The savings levers are modest but real: June and September run 10–15% cheaper than peak with excellent conditions; local markets (São Miguel's Mercado da Graça, fresh tuna from the quay in Horta) beat supermarkets on quality and price; and the archipelago's best experiences — crater-lake hikes, hot springs at Furnas, the Pico vineyards — cost little or nothing.

One honest budget note: getting there is often the biggest variable. Direct flights to Ponta Delgada from the US East Coast and mainland Europe are reasonable when booked early, but inter-island positioning flights (if your charter starts in Horta) add $100–150 per person each way.

Frequently asked questions

Are the Azores expensive?
Reasonable for the experience — cheaper than Norway, with moderate Portuguese provisioning costs.
Should I hire a skipper?
Many do, given Atlantic passages between islands — it adds only ~$215 per person on a group charter.
Any hidden costs?
Budget for a security deposit, end-of-charter cleaning, and any whale-watching excursions.
