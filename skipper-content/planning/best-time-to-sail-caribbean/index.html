Best Time to Sail the Caribbean: Season Guide for 2026

The Caribbean sailing season runs November to April — but not everywhere. Our month-by-month guide covers wind, hurricanes, and the best islands by season.

Overview

The Caribbean is a year-round destination in theory, but in practice the sailing season is firmly bounded by hurricane season. The majority of sailors visit between mid-November and late April — a window that offers the Caribbean's most reliable trade winds, lowest humidity, and most comfortable temperatures.

Peak season (December–April)

December–April is peak Caribbean sailing season. Trade winds blow steadily from the northeast at 15–25 knots; skies are blue; temperatures are 27–30°C. Christmas–New Year is the most expensive and crowded period. January through March offers the most reliable winds and the best prices outside of holidays. April sees rising temperatures and slightly less consistent winds.

Shoulder season (November, May)

November is excellent — the hurricane season is technically winding down (though not formally over until December 1st), prices drop sharply, and anchorages are quiet. May is the reverse — one of the best-value months, with trade winds still reliable and the pre-hurricane heat just arriving.

Hurricane season (June–November)

June through November is the Atlantic hurricane season. Most charter companies significantly reduce their fleets; insurance may exclude hurricane-season coverage or charge a significant premium. The southern Caribbean — Trinidad, Grenada, Bonaire, Aruba — sits south of the main hurricane track and can be sailed year-round with more confidence, though never with complete immunity.

Best islands by season

The BVI is best December–April. The Grenadines are excellent November–May. Trinidad and Tobago (south of the hurricane belt) are viable year-round. The ABC Islands (Aruba, Bonaire, Curaçao) sit outside the hurricane belt and offer year-round sailing with excellent diving.

Frequently asked questions

Is it safe to sail the Caribbean during hurricane season?
The southern Caribbean (Trinidad, Grenada, ABC islands) has a statistically lower hurricane risk. However, no part of the Caribbean is fully immune. Most experienced sailors avoid the central and northern Caribbean June–November.
When is the cheapest time to charter in the Caribbean?
May, June, and November offer the best charter rates — 20–40% below peak season prices. Just be aware of hurricane season boundaries.
Do I need hurricane coverage on my charter insurance?
Always check your charter contract and personal insurance policy. Some policies exclude hurricane damage; others offer it at a premium. It's essential if sailing outside the November–April main season.
