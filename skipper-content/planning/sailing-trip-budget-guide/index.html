Overview

A sailing holiday budget has four main components: the charter fee, provisioning, marina/anchorage fees, and travel to/from the charter base. Understanding each gives you a realistic total well before you book.

Charter fee

The charter fee is the largest single cost. A 40ft bareboat monohull split six ways in shoulder season might cost €350–€500 per person for the week — competitive with a mid-range hotel. Divide the total boat cost by your group size to get the per-person charter cost. See our full charter cost guide for 2026 pricing by boat type.

Provisioning

Budget €100–€150 per day for a group of 6 (€16–€25 per person per day). This covers breakfast, lunch, and dinner on the boat, plus drinks. Eating out for dinner at island tavernas adds €20–€40 per person per meal — allow for 3–4 restaurant meals during the week. Provisioning at the charter base supermarket is always cheaper than at island shops.

Marina fees

Marina fees vary widely: a town quay in a small Greek harbor might be free; a berth in Mykonos marina costs €80–€120 per night. Budget €30–€60/night on average, or plan an itinerary with more anchoring and less marinas to reduce this cost significantly.

Hidden costs

Don't forget: fuel (€150–€300/week), departure tax/charter permit (€100–€300), damage deposit (or waiver product at €200–€400), flights, and airport transfers. A realistic all-in budget for a Mediterranean bareboat week, per person in a group of 6, is €700–€1,200 in shoulder season.

Frequently asked questions

What is the cheapest sailing destination?
Turkey and Croatia offer the best value in the Mediterranean. The Ionian Islands in Greece are cheaper than the Cyclades. In the Caribbean, the Windward Islands are less expensive than the BVI.
How can I reduce my charter costs?
Travel in shoulder season (May, June, September), choose a monohull over a catamaran, fill the boat to maximum capacity, anchor rather than marina-berth wherever possible, and provision at the departure port supermarket.
Should I get charter insurance?
Your charter company's insurance covers major incidents but usually with a high excess. A personal yacht charter insurance policy (around €100–€200/week) reduces your liability and covers crew medical expenses.
