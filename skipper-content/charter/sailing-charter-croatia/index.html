Why charter in Croatia

Croatia has established itself as one of the best-value sailing destinations in the Mediterranean. The Dalmatian Coast's combination of 1,200 islands, crystal-clear Adriatic water, medieval harbour towns, and excellent local food and wine makes it genuinely exceptional — and the charter infrastructure (fleets, marinas, provisioning) is world-class. Prices are lower than Greece or the Balearics for comparable boats.

Bareboat charter options

Croatia has one of the largest bareboat fleets in the world. The major operators — The Moorings, Sunsail, Navigare, Ultra Sailing — all operate substantial fleets from Split and Trogir. Boats range from 32ft starter monohulls to 55ft premium catamarans. Most operators require an ICC or national equivalent and a VHF SRC; some accept a detailed experience log for smaller vessels.

Crewed charter options

Crewed yachts and gulets operate throughout Croatia, particularly in the northern Dalmatian islands. A crewed sailing yacht (skipper + hostess) starts from around €4,000–€5,000/week base for a 45ft boat. Traditional wooden Croatian brodovi — similar to Turkish gulets — offer crewed day or week charters at competitive rates.

Charter bases

Split is the main hub — excellent marinas (Marina Kaštela, Marina Baotić), international flights, and direct access to Hvar, Brač, and the southern Dalmatian islands. Trogir, 30 minutes from Split Airport, is a UNESCO World Heritage harbour town and a slightly more relaxed alternative base. Šibenik in the north offers access to the Kornati National Park archipelago.

Pricing for 2026

Bareboat monohulls: €1,500–€3,800/week. Bareboat catamarans: €3,200–€7,500/week. Peak season premium (late July–August): 25–40% above shoulder season rates. One-way charters (Split → Dubrovnik) available from most operators for a repositioning fee of €300–€800.

Frequently asked questions

What licence do I need to charter in Croatia?
An ICC (International Certificate of Competence) or national equivalent is required by Croatian maritime law, plus a VHF Short Range Certificate. These can be obtained from your national sailing authority.
Is Split or Dubrovnik better as a charter base?
Split is better — larger fleet, better provisioning, and more convenient for a week-long Dalmatian circuit. Dubrovnik is excellent as a one-way destination but has a smaller charter fleet.
Can I sail from Croatia to Montenegro or Albania?
Yes, with proper clearance. Montenegro's Bay of Kotor is a spectacular extension of a Croatian charter. Albania is more complex and requires advance planning.
