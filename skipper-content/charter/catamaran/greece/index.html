Catamaran Charter Greece: Best Routes & Prices for 2026

Greece is the world's finest catamaran charter destination — a sea of islands, reliable winds, and stunning scenery. Here's how to plan the perfect Greek catamaran holiday.

Why charter a catamaran in Greece

Greece's island geography makes it exceptional catamaran territory. Short passages between islands, protected anchorages, and long warm evenings spent in picturesque harbors are exactly what catamarans are designed for. The wide deck becomes a social hub; the twin hulls provide stability for guests unused to monohull heel; and the shallow draft opens anchorages that deeper keeled yachts can't access.

Best catamaran routes in Greece

The Ionian Islands — Lefkada, Kefalonia, Ithaka, Meganisi — are the best catamaran circuit in Greece for most groups. Winds are lighter than the Aegean, passages are short, and the anchorages are spectacularly beautiful. The Cyclades in May/June or September are equally rewarding — the meltemi hasn't yet built to its summer intensity, making for fast, comfortable sailing between iconic islands.

Pricing for 2026

A 42–45ft bareboat catamaran in Greece typically costs €3,800–€6,500/week depending on model, age, and season. Premium 48–50ft cats run €6,000–€9,500 in peak season. Add a professional skipper at €200/day and hostess at €150/day for a fully crewed experience. All prices plus running costs (provisioning, fuel, marinas).

Best time for catamaran charter in Greece

May, June, and September offer the best conditions: warm enough to swim, winds moderate enough for comfortable sailing, and significantly fewer boats in the anchorages. July and August are peak season — highest prices, busiest harbors, and the meltemi at its most energetic in the Cyclades.

Frequently asked questions

Is a catamaran better than a monohull for the Greek islands?
For groups of 6–8 people prioritizing space and comfort, yes. For couples or small groups who want the sailing experience, a monohull offers better value and more character.
Can I do a one-way catamaran charter in Greece?
Yes — most operators allow one-way charters, for example Athens to Corfu or vice versa, with a repositioning fee. Ask your charter company for one-way itinerary options.
Are there restrictions on where catamarans can anchor in Greece?
Catamarans require more swinging room than monohulls and can't use some smaller pontoons. In busy Cycladic anchorages in peak season, arriving early is essential to find space.
