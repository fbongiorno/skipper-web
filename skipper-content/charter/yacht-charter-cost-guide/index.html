What affects charter cost

Five factors drive charter pricing: boat type (monohull vs. catamaran vs. motoryacht), boat size (measured in feet/metres), destination, season, and charter type (bareboat vs. crewed). A 35ft bareboat monohull in shoulder-season Croatia costs very different from a 50ft crewed catamaran in peak-season BVI.

Bareboat pricing (2026)

Bareboat monohulls range from around €1,200–€4,000/week for a 35–45ft yacht. Factors: the newer and larger the boat, the more it costs. High season (July–August in the Med, Christmas–New Year in the Caribbean) typically adds 30–50% to shoulder-season pricing. Croatia is generally cheaper than Greece; Turkey cheaper than both.

Catamaran pricing (2026)

Bareboat catamarans start from around €3,500/week for a smaller 38–40ft cat, rising to €8,000–€12,000 for a premium 50ft+ model in peak season. Crewed catamarans (with skipper and hostess) add €700–€1,200/day to the bareboat rate.

Crewed yacht pricing (2026)

Fully crewed yacht charters — captain, chef, and crew included — start from around €5,000–€8,000/week for a smaller crewed sailing yacht, rising to €25,000+ for a 70ft+ luxury vessel. Superyacht rates run to hundreds of thousands. Most crewed charters are quoted net of APA (Advance Provisioning Allowance), which covers fuel, food, and port fees — typically 25–35% of the base rate on top.

Additional costs

  • Provisioning: €80–€150/day for 6–8 people
  • Marina fees: €20–€100/night depending on location and marina quality
  • Fuel: €150–€400 per week depending on motoring
  • Damage deposit: €1,500–€5,000 (or use a deposit waiver product)
  • Charter permit/tax: varies by country (€100–€300 for most European destinations)
  • Skipper hire: €150–€250/day if required or desired

How to save money

Book early (6+ months) for the best selection. Travel in shoulder season (May, June, September, October) for significant savings. Choose a monohull over a catamaran if space isn't the priority. Consider newer-but-smaller over larger-but-older boats — newer boats tend to be better maintained. Join a flotilla for built-in support at charter rates.

Frequently asked questions

Is VAT included in charter prices?
Usually not. Most European charter prices are quoted ex-VAT, which then applies at the local rate (typically 13–25% depending on country). Always check whether the quoted price is inclusive or exclusive of tax.
What is a damage deposit and how can I avoid it?
A refundable damage deposit (€1,500–€5,000) is held by the charter company against accidental damage. You can avoid locking up this cash by purchasing a damage deposit waiver product through the charter company or a specialist broker — typically €200–€400/week.
Is it cheaper to book direct with a charter company or through a broker?
Brokers rarely add cost to the charter price — they're paid commission by the charter company. For crewed yachts and flotillas, a good broker adds significant value in selection and support. For straightforward bareboat charters, booking direct is fine.
