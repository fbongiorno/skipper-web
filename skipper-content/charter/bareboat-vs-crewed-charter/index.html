Bareboat Charter vs. Crewed Charter: Which is Right for You?

The choice between bareboat and crewed charter shapes your entire holiday. Here's how to decide — honestly.

What is bareboat charter?

A bareboat charter means you hire the boat without crew — you are the skipper. You're responsible for the navigation, anchoring, provisioning, and safety of your group. It offers maximum flexibility and independence: go where you want, when you want, eat what you like, and set your own pace. It's the most popular form of sailing holiday worldwide for experienced sailors.

What is crewed charter?

A crewed charter means the boat comes with a professional captain (and often a chef/hostess). You're a guest, not the skipper. The crew handles all navigation, sail handling, and boat management while you relax, explore, and enjoy. It's the sailing equivalent of a boutique hotel with a private skipper — and priced accordingly.

Cost comparison

A bareboat charter costs the base boat rate plus your own costs (provisioning, fuel, marina fees). A crewed charter costs the base rate plus crew fees (typically €700–€1,500/day for skipper and hostess), plus APA (Advance Provisioning Allowance) of 25–35% on top. On a like-for-like basis, a crewed charter costs roughly 2–3x a bareboat on the same yacht. The offset: zero work for you, and a significantly higher quality of on-board experience.

Experience required

Bareboat requires a sailing qualification and demonstrable experience. Crewed requires only a budget — anyone can enjoy a crewed charter regardless of sailing experience.

Which is right for you?

Choose bareboat if you're a qualified sailor, value independence and the hands-on sailing experience, and want the best value for money. Choose crewed if you're celebrating a special occasion, travelling with non-sailors, want a genuinely luxurious experience, or simply don't want to do any sailing work yourself. Many experienced sailors do both — bareboat with sailing friends, crewed when travelling with family.

Frequently asked questions

Can I hire a skipper for a bareboat?
Yes — most charter companies can arrange a freelance skipper for €150–€250/day. This gives you the independence of a bareboat charter with professional support.
Do I tip the crew on a crewed charter?
Tipping is customary and expected — typically 10–15% of the base charter fee for excellent service, given in cash to the captain at the end of the trip.
Can beginners do a bareboat charter?
Technically yes if you meet the company's experience requirements, but a flotilla charter (guided group) is a better option for less experienced sailors — you have your own boat but professional support available.
