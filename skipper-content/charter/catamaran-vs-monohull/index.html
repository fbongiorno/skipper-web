Catamaran vs. Monohull Charter: Which Should You Choose?

The catamaran vs. monohull debate is the first decision most charter sailors face. Both are excellent — for different reasons. Here's the honest comparison.

Space & comfort

Catamarans win on space — decisively. A 45ft catamaran offers roughly twice the living area of a 45ft monohull: a large cockpit for dining, a spacious saloon, and typically four double cabins with private heads. The wide, flat deck creates a usable outdoor space unlike anything a monohull offers. For families, groups of 6–8, or anyone prioritizing comfort over sailing purity, the catamaran's space advantage is significant.

Sailing performance

Monohulls generally offer a better pure sailing experience — they heel, respond to the helm more intuitively, and reward skilled boat handling. Catamarans are faster on most points of sail (especially reaching and downwind), but their large beam makes them unwieldy in marinas and limited to designated catamaran berths. In strong winds, a catamaran's speed advantage grows; in light air, they can be frustratingly slow.

Stability

Catamarans are dramatically more stable than monohulls — they don't heel significantly even in strong winds. This makes them ideal for guests who get seasick easily, for children, and for anyone who prioritizes platform stability over sailing sensation. Monohulls heel to 20–30° in a good breeze; some guests find this uncomfortable initially, though most adapt quickly.

Cost

Catamarans typically cost 40–80% more than equivalent monohulls to charter. A 45ft monohull might cost €2,500–€4,000/week; an equivalent catamaran €4,500–€7,000. The per-person cost is similar if you fill a catamaran (8 berths vs. 6), but the headline price is significantly higher. Fuel costs can also be higher as twin engines are used more in catamarans.

When to choose a catamaran

Choose a cat if: you have 6–8 people aboard, someone in your crew is prone to seasickness, you're chartering in a hot climate and want shade and deck space, you're doing a Caribbean trade-wind route, or comfort and living space are the priority.

When to choose a monohull

Choose a monohull if: you have fewer than 6 people, you want the "real" sailing experience with heel and spray, you're on a budget, you want flexibility in marina berths, or you're chartering in the Mediterranean where light airs make catamaran speed advantages less relevant.

Frequently asked questions

Are catamarans safer than monohulls?
Catamarans are more stable and less likely to capsize in normal conditions. However, a capsized catamaran is harder to recover than a monohull, which tends to self-right. Both are safe within their design parameters.
Can beginners charter a catamaran?
Yes, but the larger beam requires more care in marinas and anchorages. Most charter companies ask for slightly more experience (or a skipper) for cats. The sailing itself is straightforward.
Why are catamarans so much more expensive to charter?
Higher purchase price, higher maintenance costs, and limited berth availability (dedicated catamaran pontoons are in short supply at many marinas) all push charter prices up.
