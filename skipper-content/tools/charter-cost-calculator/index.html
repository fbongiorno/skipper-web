Frequently asked questions

How accurate are these charter cost estimates?
They're realistic planning ranges built from typical charter rates and running costs in each destination. Actual quotes vary by boat age, model, and demand — always confirm with a charter operator before budgeting.
What's not included in the estimate?
A refundable security deposit (often $1,500–$4,000 on bareboat), the optional damage waiver, flights, and crew gratuity on crewed charters (customarily 5–15% of the fee).
What local taxes and permits will I have to pay?
It varies a lot by country. Croatia charges a transit log of roughly EUR 3-5 per person per day; Greece has the TEPAI cruising tax and DEKPA transit log; the BVI charges a cruising permit per person per day plus National Parks fees; the Bahamas charges a cruising permit plus a passenger tax; and the Maldives levies a green tax of $6 per guest per night. The calculator estimates these, but rates change often — always confirm with your operator.
Why does a crewed charter cost so much more?
You're paying professional salaries for a skipper and cook/host who work your entire trip. Split across a full group, the per-person premium is often smaller than expected.
Is it cheaper to sail outside peak season?
Usually 15–30% cheaper. Shoulder months offer the same boats at lower rates, with quieter anchorages — the trade-off is slightly less settled weather.
